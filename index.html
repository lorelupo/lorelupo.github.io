Lorenzo Lupo

I am an interdisciplinary researcher with expertise in machine learning and natural language processing applied to the social sciences, with a particular interest in the societal impacts of AI.
My recent work includes evaluating the influence of AI on knowledge platforms such as Wikipedia, investigating LLMs' simulation capabilities of entire demographic groups, and analyzing legal and social media texts to uncover historical trends and shifts in national cultures.
After completing my PhD on neural machine translation at Grenoble Alpes University, I held research positions at the University of Milan and Bocconi University, where I currently work as a freelance researcher, collaborating with Kai Zhu, Nicolò Cavalli, the MilanNLP research group, and many other researchers.
